Bayesian estimates and weekly predictions of COVID-19 in Uppsala, Sweden

    Conference: Epidemics8 - 8th International Conference on Infectious Disease Dynamics

    Download Poster

    Abstract:

    In October 2020, amid the COVID-19 second wave, the cross-disciplinary project CRUSH Covid started at Uppsala University, Sweden, involving more than 40 researchers at five different departments. The project aims to support the local public healthcare authorities in the decision-making regarding pandemic mitigation response. The project publishes weekly reports that summarize the findings of the project group, based on healthcare system information, sewage water analysis, and symptoms monitoring smartphone application data. Furthermore, within the CRUSH Covid effort, our group develops a compartmental mathematical model and uses it for predicting future hospitalization needs.

    We center the model around publicly available hospitalization data: in-patient care, intensive care, and reported deaths. The model is an extension of the SEIR model that incorporates said data in distinct compartments. In a simulation scenario, one usually interprets the transitions between the compartments as a continuous-time Markov chain. However, a more practical model form is a discretized linear diffusion that one can formally derive from the Markov chain as a system's size limit. The main benefit of this modeling choice is that one can optimally estimate the model states by a Kalman filter, which then allows for parameterization using the filter's marginal likelihood estimate in conjunction with an adaptive Metropolis sampler.

    The week-by-week inference is run on a personal computer and results in Bayesian credible intervals for, but not limited to:

  • A per-month estimate of the Reproduction number
  • A 7-day ahead prediction for hospital demand An estimation of the total number of individuals that have contracted the disease
  • Finally, we also assess the robustness of our results through a synthetic data setup.
